Modeling the Central Bank's response to Exchange Rate Volatility under an Inflation-Targeting Regime using conditional volatility models such as ARIMA and GARCH.
Click here for pdf of the final report.

Learning Outcomes

  • Exchange Rates have a high pass-through to inflation. Therefore, the modeling and forecast of exchange rates and their volatility is key to price stability.
  • Under an inflation-targeting regime, the Central Bank has to be cautious about its intervention in the forex market, for interventions signal expectations of future rates/prices.
  • Further, under an IT regime, the Central Bank's credibility depends on its primary goal of inflation target, and the curbing of excessive volatility is but secondary.
  • Using GARCH models, a rolling forecast about the exchange rate volatilites have been made in this analysis. Further scope for research include accounting for asymmetric effects of economic shocks and extending this to a mulitvariate model.
